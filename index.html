About

Algothon 2018 in partnership with BlackRock is the inaugural event of the 2018/19 academic year organised by the Imperial College Algorithmic Trading Society. We’re excited to bring you 24 hours of intense hacking, bringing 200 of the brightest minds together to produce incredible new financial technology.

The event will be held in the offices of BlackRock, one of the most highly respected financial technology companies operating today. In addition to the main event, we’re delighted to offer several talks from field leading researchers in machine learning, as well as workshops to get you up to speed on some of the most exciting new technologies and practices in the field.

This will be an incredible weekend, producing amazing new technologies, learning from some of the best minds in the field, and overall having a great time!

Activities

Algothon

In the titular event, you will have 24 hours to produce the most innovative algorithm you can. At the end of the 24 hours, all teams will have the opportunity to demonstrate their product in a fair where the judges (and other participants) will go around and see what has been produced. The judges will select their top 6 entrants, who will then be able to give a short presentation to all attendees to showcase their work. Note that this is not only a test of technical performance. For instance, a team who’s product isn’t working well technically but who has a practical plan of implementing it in a real world situation has just as much chance of winning as a team with a well made technical product but no plan for implementing it. The judging panel will consist of world-leading academics.

Datathon

This is a smaller event which will be running alongside the main Algothon. This will be a test of pure data science. We will be providing a dataset and some metadata (e.g. all of Trump’s tweets, some financial news stories, and many other items) and participants must predict how the dataset will behave in the future. This will be a purely objective competition, testing skills in natural language processing, stochastic analysis and any other technologies you can bring to the field. The submission with the closest prediction to actuality will win. There will be a leaderboard updated every 3 hours throughout the hack.

Workshops

We will be running several workshops that we hope will give participants some helpful information on subjects that are unfamiliar to them.

Introduction to Data Science with Python

There is no requirement for your submission to use Python, however as it is one of the most popular languages currently in use for data science, we hope this workshop will get you up to speed on using Python in this context. The workshop will go over the basic syntax of Python, some useful features for working with data, and some of the most popular libraries (NumPy and Pandas). Though we expect that participants in attendance are relatively skilled programmers, we do recommend familiarising yourself with Python before attending this workshop.

Basic Machine Learning with Python

One of the most popular machine learning tools available right now is Tensorflow. This workshop will get you up and running with some sample data, producing a model, and applying it to get some useful results. This will build upon the previous workshop, however it will be a big jump up if you are new to Python. Before attending, we would recommend installing Tensorflow and running through some examples to ensure that it is working properly. This will not be a workshop covering the theory behind machine learning, so it will be helpful to familiarise yourself with the basics of the topic.

Code of Conduct


TL;DR. Be respectful to all involved in or present at the event. Harassment and abuse are never tolerated. If you are in a situation that makes you uncomfortable at this event or directly in relation to this event, if the event itself is creating an unsafe or inappropriate environment, or if interacting with any person(s) involved in or present at the event makes you uncomfortable, please report it using the procedures under this section.

We believe that every single person has the right to participate and hack in a safe and welcoming environment.

Harassment includes but is not limited to offensive verbal or written comments related to gender, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, social class, economic status, veteran status, sexual images, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention. If what you’re doing is making someone feel uncomfortable, that counts as harassment and is enough reason to stop doing it.

Any person(s) involved in or present at the event asked to stop any harassing behaviour are expected to comply immediately.

Judges, mentors, volunteers, organizers, staff, and anyone else involved in or present at the event are also subject to the anti-harassment policy. In particular, attendees should not use sexualised images, activities, or other material both in their hacks and during the event. Staff (including volunteers) should not use sexualised clothing/uniforms/costumes, or otherwise create a sexualised environment.

If a participant engages in harassing behaviour, the organizers may take any action it deems appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the event with no eligibility for reimbursement or refund of any type.

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact a member of the organizing committee present at the event either in person or through email to the committee.

The organizing committee will be happy to help participants contact campus security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the event. We value your attendance.

We expect participants to follow these rules at the event venue, online interactions in relation to the event, event-related social events, and on supplied transportation if any.

Reporting Procedures

If you feel uncomfortable or think there may be a potential violation of the code of conduct, please report it immediately to a member of the organizing committee present at the event or through email to the organizing committee at algo.trade@imperial.ac.uk. All reporters have the right to remain anonymous.

Terms and Conditions

The organizers reserve the right to revise, make exceptions to, or otherwise amend these policies in whole or in part. If you have any questions regarding the code of conduct for this event, please contact the organizing committee by email at algo.trade@imperial.ac.uk.

FAQs

Q. What is an Algothon?

An algothon is a hackathon with a focus on financial algorithms and data science.

For the main event, you will have 24 hours to create the most promising trading strategy you possibly can. This is an amazing event for learning new skills and having a lot of fun!


Q. Who can attend?

The event is open to current University students (from any University), recent graduates, and academics. Send us an email or Facebook message if you are not sure whether you can attend.


Q. What about under 18s?

Unfortunately we are unable to allow under 18s to attend.


Q. What does it cost?

The event is totally free to attend!


Q. Can I stay overnight?

Of course. There will be a unisex room put aside for participants to sleep in, although you will have to bring your own blankets/sleeping bag/pillows.


Q. What should I bring?

Please bring your laptop and charger as well as anything else that you need think you will need for a weekend of design and programming (headphones, favourite mug, etc.). However, participants should not bring along anything that might distract other attendees (bluetooth speakers etc.).


Q. What is the maximum team size?

Teams are to have a maximum of 4 members.


Q. I don't have a team, can I make one on the day?

Prior to the start of the hack, there will be a short networking/team building session which will give participants the opportunity to join an existing team or form a new team.


Q. Can I continue with a project which I have already started?

To make the event fair for all participants, you will not be eligible for any prizes for projects that have been started prior to the event. You are welcome to have an idea and/or plans for what you want to make, but no code can be written before the start of the hack.


Q. Do we have to use Python in the Algothon?

Not at all, feel free to use whatever language you want for the competition. As long as your results and methods are obvious then there is no problem.


Q. Do we retain ownership of anything we make during the Algothon?

Of course, anything you create during the Algothon is your personal intellectual property.


Q. Can you help me set up Python/Tensorflow/other tool?

We will do our best to help with technical issues but we encourage you to have everything installed and tested before arriving. This is especially important for larger packages like Tensorflow, whereby if all 200 participants attempted to download it at the same time then there will be network problems.


Q. What food is available? I have dietary restrictions.

We will provide lunch, dinner, and a midnight snack on Saturday. There will be breakfast and lunch provided on Sunday. Snacks (various crisps, chocolate bars, fruits etc.) and drinks (tea, coffee, soft drinks, water etc.) will be available throughout the whole weekend. We will endeavour to ensure that the food ordered is suitable for as many people as possible, but please let us know beforehand if you have any dietary restrictions so that we can ensure that you are catered for.


Q. Is travel reimbursed?

We are hoping to reimburse a small amount for travel from outside of London, but unfortunately we will not be able to reimburse food or accommodation costs. Please bring any travel receipts along with you to the event.


Q. What prizes are available?

This will be confirmed on the day of the event, but we are planning for some really cool stuff to be presented to winners.


Q. How do I get to the event?

Please check the Details section.
