About

Algothon 2018 in partnership with BlackRock is the inaugural event of the 2018/19 academic year organised by the Imperial College Algorithmic Trading Society. We’re excited to bring you 24 hours of intense hacking, bringing 200 of the brightest minds together to produce incredible new financial technology.

The event will be held in the offices of BlackRock, one of the most highly respected financial technology companies operating today. In addition to the main event, we’re delighted to offer several talks from field leading researchers in machine learning, as well as workshops to get you up to speed on some of the most exciting new technologies and practices in the field.

This will be an incredible weekend, producing amazing new technologies, learning from some of the best minds in the field, and overall having a great time!

Activities

Algothon

In the titular event, you will have 24 hours to produce the most innovative algorithm you can. At the end of the 24 hours, all teams will have the opportunity to demonstrate their product in a fair where the judges (and other participants) will go around and see what has been produced. The judges will select their top 6 entrants, who will then be able to give a short presentation to all attendees to showcase their work. Note that this is not only a test of technical performance. For instance, a team who’s product isn’t working well technically but who has a practical plan of implementing it in a real world situation has just as much chance of winning as a team with a well made technical product but no plan for implementing it. The judging panel will consist of world-leading academics.

Datathon

This is a smaller event which will be running alongside the main Algothon. This will be a test of pure data science. We will be providing a dataset and some metadata (e.g. all of Trump’s tweets, some financial news stories, and many other items) and participants must predict how the dataset will behave in the future. This will be a purely objective competition, testing skills in natural language processing, stochastic analysis and any other technologies you can bring to the field. The submission with the closest prediction to actuality will win. There will be a leaderboard updated every 3 hours throughout the hack.

Workshops

We will be running several workshops that we hope will give participants some helpful information on subjects that are unfamiliar to them.

Introduction to Data Science with Python

There is no requirement for your submission to use Python, however as it is one of the most popular languages currently in use for data science, we hope this workshop will get you up to speed on using Python in this context. The workshop will go over the basic syntax of Python, some useful features for working with data, and some of the most popular libraries (NumPy and Pandas). Though we expect that participants in attendance are relatively skilled programmers, we do recommend familiarising yourself with Python before attending this workshop.

Basic Machine Learning with Python

One of the most popular machine learning tools available right now is Tensorflow. This workshop will get you up and running with some sample data, producing a model, and applying it to get some useful results. This will build upon the previous workshop, however it will be a big jump up if you are new to Python. Before attending, we would recommend installing Tensorflow and running through some examples to ensure that it is working properly. This will not be a workshop covering the theory behind machine learning, so it will be helpful to familiarise yourself with the basics of the topic.

FAQs

Q. Do we have to use Python in the Algothon?

Not at all, feel free to use whatever tool you want for the competition. As long as your results and methods are obvious to someone without expertise in the tool you are using then there is no problem.


Q. Can you help me set up Python/Tensorflow/other tool?

We’ll do our best to help with whatever technical issues you may be having, but bear in mind that none of us are true experts on the subject and we’d encourage you to have everything installed before arriving. This is especially true for larger packages like Tensorflow, if all 200 participants attempted to download it at the same time then we might start to have some network problems.


Q. What food is available? I have dietary restrictions

We will supply lunch, dinner, and a midnight snack on Saturday. Sunday will have breakfast and lunch supplied. Snacks (various crisps, chocolate bars, fruit etc.) and drinks (tea, coffee, soft drinks, water etc.) will be available over the whole weekend. We will endeavour to ensure that the food ordered is suitable for as many people as possible, but please let us know if you have any dietary restrictions so that we can ensure you’re catered for.


Q. Is travel reimbursed?

We are hoping to reimburse a small amount for travel from outside London, but unfortunately we can’t reimburse food or accommodation costs. Please bring any travel receipts along with you to the event.


Q. What prizes are available?

This will be confirmed on the day of the event, but we’re hoping for some really cool stuff to be available to the winners. This will include an interview with Blackrock for their internship program, skipping several application stages!
